## Summary
This markdown file is a landing page for the Declarative DevOps Microframeworks series rather than a standalone essay. It organizes the series into conceptual articles and applied walkthroughs, and makes explicit that functional programming sits adjacent to the core series even though it is not counted as one of the central declarative-devops chapters. In the wiki, it mainly serves as a structural source for how the series is intended to be read.

## Key Claims
- The series is deliberately split into concept-building articles and application walkthroughs.
- Functional programming is treated as a supporting prerequisite for understanding many of the series articles.
- Private modules, maintainability, config management, idempotency, and work-item automation are presented as concrete applications of the microframework philosophy.

## Key Quotes
> "Declarative DevOps Microframeworks is the most robust, portable, and scalable pattern for managing your DevOps systems."
